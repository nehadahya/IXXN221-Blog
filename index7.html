Week Seven: 05/09/2023

Composition

During today’s lecture we learned about composition. This included looking at grids and the layout of webpages, visual hierarchy, colour and typography, scale, white space, and balance. I now understand the importance of grids, as they provide a useful means when thinking about page components and how it flows. I need to consider this when it comes to designing my website and placing elements onto the page to ensure the flow of content makes sense. Visual hierarchy is also an extremely important part of web design as it can help grab users' attention and guide them through the website in an appropriate/intended order. I learned how visual hierarchy can be achieved through a variety of elements. This includes how the size and weight of text and contrast of colour can grab attention by making elements appear more popped out. After learning about colour and typography in a previous lecture, this has made me feel more confident in applying it to my design. Scale is also important for visual hierarchy, as it determines the order of importance of elements on a page. White space allows elements to ‘breathe’ on a page, and can draw user’s eyes towards imagery, text, call-to-actions, and certain colour, which is something I will utilise in my designs. When creating designs for my website I will consider how I use hierarchy and the elements listed above in order to apply composition, which will help create an effective website and easy navigation for the user. Because my website is targeted to people who are anxious/unorganised, I want to ensure I properly apply these elements/principles to ensure my website provides a simple and calming experience.



Affordance

This lecture also went over affordance. I was introduced to the mental model, which allowed me to understand how people may construct these based on past interactions and beliefs. This means people can construct different mental models of the same user interface, which is an important point, yet something I have never thought about before. Mental models of website structures are formed so a user has expectations when navigating through a page. Affordances show how users interact with certain elements on a website, such as buttons, links, input fields, and icons. Affordances can provide useful cues and psychological shortcuts for a user, which is important to consider. Call-to-actions are a crucial element of a website and are often displayed using buttons that redirect a user. The affordance is when the button isclicked, it will lead the user to the relevant page. Colours, shapes, and text of a button are helpful indicators of affordance and establish the importance of it and provide clear communication for a user, which is important so they clearly understand the website. This is important for my design as its crucial users avoid confusion and frustration. I am certain I will have affordances such as buttons and links on my website, so it is important I carefully consider the layout as well as the different elements such as button colour and size. This will ensure affordance can be applied in order to benefit the design and create a positive user experience.
